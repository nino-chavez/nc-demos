Amendment to the decision-bias rule: for architecture, contract, or taxonomy design decisions, generating 2-3 competing candidate structures and comparing them against real cases before committing to one is not hesitation — it is the work. Producing a single draft and asking for a tear-apart does not satisfy this; the alternatives must each be developed enough to be compared. The anti-hesitation rule governs pace, not convergence: do not collapse design divergence prematurely to appear decisive.
