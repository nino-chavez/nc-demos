Agree with throwing it out — but the reason matters. The current IA is organized by what the methodology *produces* (research, decisions, feedback, ADRs, portal chrome), not by what a reader *needs to do*. Iterating on that skeleton just rearranges an artifact taxonomy. No amount of polish fixes an IA whose organizing principle is the pipeline's internal structure.

First principles, starting from your own questions:

**Who actually lands on the film-room portal?** Realistically two readers: you (resuming context, judging state) and maybe a 630 stakeholder deciding "does this do what I need for tomorrow's clips?" That's it. There is no exec/PM/engineer trio here. The three-persona switcher is inherited from the self-application portal, where multiple audiences were real because the methodology itself has consumers, contributors, and evaluators. Stamped onto a single-operator initiative, it's a costume with nobody inside it. Kill it. Write for the one primary reader; personas should be derived from who actually opens the link, not shipped as default chrome.

**What's the job?** For a consumer initiative portal, the reader questions are:

1. What is this and who is it for — one screen, no methodology vocabulary.
2. What works today — the demo/output front and center. For film-room that's processed clips, not a stage ladder.
3. What's decided and what it means for me — plain-language decision digest with consequences, not raw ADRs. ADRs are for the build team; link them below the fold for whoever digs. "Internal speak" is the correct register for the ADR file and the wrong register for the portal surface — the fix is a translation layer, not rewriting the ADRs.
4. What's open / what I need from you — the asks.
5. Evidence, for readers who want to verify — research lives here, last.

Everything in the current IA either maps to one of those five or gets cut.

**Two guardrails on the redesign:**

- Apply your own design-bar rule first: if film-room's portal is only ever read by you, most of this is over-built regardless of IA. The tier ladder (`docs/portal-and-tier-ladder.md`) already exists for exactly this — film-room may be on the wrong tier more than the wrong IA. Check that before redesigning.
- Don't redesign inside film-room as a one-off. Design the reader-question IA in film-room as the pilot, then promote it as a wave to `template/apps/portal/` — same dogfood-then-promote path every other methodology change has taken. A bespoke film-room portal that diverges from template recreates the leak the charter warns about, and the fourth initiative hits this same wall again.

The concrete next step: write the five reader questions as the portal spec, audit film-room's current pages against them, and cut everything that doesn't answer one. That audit output becomes the wave brief for the template redesign.
