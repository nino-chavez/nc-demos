Agree on the throw-out, with one scoping correction: the thing to throw out is the template's portal IA, not film-room's instance. Redefining it per-initiative recreates the leak the charter warns about — bespoke portal forks that drift from the harness. The evidence you're citing (same "feels off" across initiatives) is the methodology's own signal: repeated per-instance struggle means the capability is missing at the source. So this is a wave against `template/`, argued in an ADR, not another film-room pass.

**The actual job to be done.** A portal exists for exactly one reader: someone who wasn't in the sessions and needs to trust or use the output. For film-room that's two people, not three personas — a 630 stakeholder deciding "does this clips pipeline work and how do I get my film," and future-you resuming state. Both want the same first screen, which is why the switcher feels like fluff: audience-branching only earns its cost when audiences need *different* first screens. Here they don't. The switcher was inherited from a consulting-deliverable assumption (exec/PM/engineer reading the same brief differently) that none of your current initiatives actually have.

**First-principles IA, three layers, in order of who arrives:**

1. **The artifact, working.** A processed clip playing above the fold. For any initiative: the prototype itself, not a description of it. The portal's credibility is the demo, because the reader's real question is "is this real."
2. **Current state.** What's decided, what's in flight, what's pending — the resume-context layer. This is for the operator and for a returning stakeholder. It's derivable from `blueprint.yml` + decisions, so it should be generated, not authored.
3. **Evidence on demand.** Research and decisions, but translated. ADRs read as internal speak because they *are* internal — they're written for agents and future sessions. The portal shouldn't render them; it should render "what this decision changed about what you get," one line each, linking to the raw ADR for the rare reader who wants it. Surfacing raw ADRs was the document-centric assumption: portal-as-filing-cabinet instead of portal-as-proof.

The current IA leads with layer 3 and gates it behind layer 0 (self-classification). Invert it.

**On process:** before the redesign wave, pull the one input a blank page can't give you — the `feedback/` captures across initiatives. What did Travis, the 630 folks, and the subs stakeholders actually ask after seeing a portal? Those questions are the IA. Redesigning from first principles without them just swaps one guess for a newer guess. That's the next move I'd take: mine the captures, write the IA ADR at `tools/blueprint`, restamp film-room as the first consumer of the new shell.
